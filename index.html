About Me

Born and raised in St. Louis, Missouri, Sam Altman's journey into technology began at the age of 8 when he received his first computer. Despite dropping out of Stanford University, his entrepreneurial spirit led him to found Loopt, a location-based social networking app, at just 19 years old.

In 2014, Sam became the President of Y Combinator, one of the world's most prestigious startup accelerators. Under his leadership, YC expanded significantly, launching initiatives like YC Continuity and Startup School to support founders at various stages.

In 2015, Sam co-founded OpenAI with a mission to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. As CEO, he has overseen the development of revolutionary AI systems like ChatGPT and DALL-E, transforming how we interact with technology.

Sam is openly gay and a signatory of the Giving Pledge, committing to donate the majority of his wealth to charitable causes during his lifetime.

November 2023: Brief Ousting and Reinstatement at OpenAI

In one of the most dramatic corporate events in tech history, Sam was briefly removed as CEO of OpenAI before being reinstated days later following overwhelming support from employees and the tech community.

Projects & Ventures

OpenAI

Co-founded in 2015, OpenAI is dedicated to ensuring that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. Under Sam's leadership, OpenAI has developed groundbreaking AI systems like ChatGPT and DALL-E.

Y Combinator

As President from 2014 to 2019, Sam transformed Y Combinator into a global powerhouse for startup acceleration. He launched YC Continuity, a fund for growth-stage companies, and Startup School, an online program for aspiring founders.

Key Investments

Sam has made significant investments in transformative companies, including Helion (fusion energy), Stripe (payments infrastructure), and Reddit (social news aggregation). His investment philosophy focuses on companies with potential for exponential impact.
